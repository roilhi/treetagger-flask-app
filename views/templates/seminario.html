Seminario Permanente de LCR

The creation of a learner corpus is only the first step. To fully benefit from its research potential, users need the methodological and analytical skills required to explore and interpret corpus data. The Seminar Series was created to support this process through regular training and discussion sessions within a collaborative research community.

Academic orientation

The Seminar Series is a pedagogically oriented academic space dedicated to learner corpus research. Its aim is to promote methodological literacy, strengthen analytical skills and facilitate engagement with both foundational and current discussions in the field.
Contributions are not expected to present novel or unpublished research. Sessions focusing on corpus resources, research methods, analytical techniques and established approaches are particularly welcome. Emphasis is placed on clarity, transparency and replicability in order to support research training and collective development within the community.
The seminar is open to anyone interested in learning about learner corpus research, regardless of their level of expertise. Sessions are held bimonthly and organised on a semester basis. To maximise accessibility and regional impact, Spanish is the preferred language of the Seminar Series, although presentations may also be delivered in English.

Format

Each session is delivered by an invited or selected specialist and structured as an advanced seminar. Speakers are expected to:

  • Anchor the session in a key reading or conceptual framework
  • Make methodological reasoning explicit
  • Engage directly with learner corpus data
  • Facilitate structured academic discussion

The Laboratory welcomes proposals from scholars working in learner corpus research, interlanguage studies, corpus linguistics and related analytical domains.

Selection criteria

Submit a session proposal

Proposals are reviewed in accordance with the following principles:

  • Relevance to learner corpus methodology or learner language analysis
  • Methodological clarity and analytical transparency
  • Demonstrable didactic value for an academically engaged but non-specialist audience
  • Meaningful engagement with empirical data
  • Alignment with the Laboratory’s mission to strengthen research capacity in the region

Participation in the Seminar Series implies a commitment to contributing to a growing scholarly community dedicated to the responsible and sustained development of learner corpus research.
