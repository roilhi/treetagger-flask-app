Seminario Permanente de LCR

The Laboratory was established on the premise that data alone does not create knowledge: scholarly communities do. The Mexican Learner Corpus 3.0 (MexLeC) is conceived as an open research resource, yet its long-term significance depends on cultivating researchers who are methodologically equipped to analyse learner language with rigour, critical awareness and analytical confidence. The Seminario Permanente de LCR series therefore functions as a community-building and capacity-strengthening platform, with particular attention to Mexico and Latin America, where learner corpus research is an emerging and consolidating field. Its purpose is to contribute to the gradual expansion of disciplinary expertise in the region through structured knowledge-sharing and collaborative engagement.

Academic orientation

The Seminar Series is designed as a pedagogically grounded academic space rather than a conventional conference venue. It does not prioritise novelty for its own sake, nor does it seek to replicate highly specialised research environments. Instead, it focuses on strengthening methodological literacy, clarifying analytical procedures and facilitating access to established and state-of-the-art discussions in the field. Proposals are not required to present new or unpublished research. Sessions that revisit foundational frameworks, consolidate core methodologies and make complex debates accessible are particularly valued. The emphasis lies on explanation, transparency and replicability. Contributors are invited to approach the seminar as an opportunity for collective advancement. The guiding principle is contribution rather than display, capacity-building rather than academic performance. Given this regional mission, Spanish is the preferred language of the Seminar Series in order to maximise accessibility and research training impact although sessions may also be conducted in English.

Format

Each session is delivered by an invited or selected specialist and structured as an advanced seminar. Speakers are expected to:

  • Anchor the session in a key reading or conceptual framework
  • Make methodological reasoning explicit
  • Engage directly with learner corpus data
  • Facilitate structured academic discussion

The Laboratory welcomes proposals from scholars working in learner corpus research, interlanguage studies, corpus linguistics and related analytical domains.

Selection criteria

Submit a session proposal

Proposals are reviewed in accordance with the following principles:

  • Relevance to learner corpus methodology or learner language analysis
  • Methodological clarity and analytical transparency
  • Demonstrable didactic value for an academically engaged but non-specialist audience
  • Meaningful engagement with empirical data
  • Alignment with the Laboratory’s mission to strengthen research capacity in the region

Participation in the Seminar Series implies a commitment to contributing to a growing scholarly community dedicated to the responsible and sustained development of learner corpus research.
