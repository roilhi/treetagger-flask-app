LCR Lab: Learner Corpus Research Laboratory

LCR Lab is an independent research initiative dedicated to the design, development, and analysis of learner corpora and linguistically informed digital tools. The laboratory integrates corpus compilation, morphological database construction, computational processing, and methodological innovation within a coherent research framework.

Its work combines theoretical linguistics, corpus methodology, and applied computational approaches to examine morphological structure, lexical development, and emergent patterns in learner language. Particular attention is given to database curation, annotation alignment, and the systematic modelling of linguistic information for research and pedagogical purposes.

The laboratory currently hosts three core developments:

  • MexLeC 3.0, a longitudinal Mexican Learner Corpus
  • Morph 1.0, a morphological analysis tool and database
  • Seminario Permanente de LCR, a research seminar series devoted to disseminate Learner Corpus Research methodology
